## 1. The Pre-Pay Hack (Save up to 10% Instantly)

The easiest way to save on Lovevery is through their official subscription model. While paying kit-by-kit is the most flexible, pre-paying for a 3-kit or 4-kit bundle offers an automatic discount.

*   **Standard Kit Price:** ~$80 per kit (for infant kits)
*   **3-Kit Bundle:** Save roughly 5%
*   **4-Kit Bundle:** Save roughly 10%

While 10% isn't 40%, this is the baseline savings you should always start with if you plan on keeping the subscription for at least 6 months.

## 2. Use the "Refer-a-Friend" Program

Lovevery has one of the most generous referral programs in the baby gear space. In 2026, current subscribers can often send $20 or $40 discounts to friends.

**How to find a code:**
- Check parenting Facebook groups.
- Search "Lovevery referral thread" on Reddit (r/beyondthebump is a gold mine).
- Ask friends who already have the kits.

By using a $40 referral code on your first bundle, you can often reach that **40% savings mark** on your initial investment.

## 3. The Amazon Registry Trick (15% Off)

If you are currently expecting or have a baby under 6 months old, you can use the **Amazon Baby Registry Completion Discount**.

1.  Add Lovevery Play Kits (or the Play Gym) to your Amazon Baby Registry.
2.  Wait until you are within 60 days of your due date.
3.  Apply your 15% Completion Discount to the Lovevery items.

*Note: Lovevery often limits which kits are available on Amazon, but the Play Gym and the Block Set are almost always eligible for the discount.*

## 4. Buy Used and Resell (The "Net Zero" Strategy)

This is the secret to saving the most money—potentially even 100%. Lovevery toys have incredible resale value. In 2026, "Lovevery Buy/Sell/Trade" groups are more active than ever.

**The Strategy:**
1.  **Buy a used kit** on Facebook Marketplace or Mercari for 40-50% off retail.
2.  **Use it** for the 2-3 months your child is in that developmental stage.
3.  **Resell it** for exactly what you paid (or just $10 less).

Because the toys are made of solid wood and organic cotton, they rarely "wear out." Many parents find that after reselling, their net cost per kit was less than $20.

## 5. Target the "Subscription Remainder" Sales

Sometimes, Lovevery partners with retailers like Target to sell individual items from kits. While you don't get the full kit, you can often find the "hero" toys (like the wooden car or the felt ball run) on clearance in the Target toy aisle or on their website.

## 6. Stork Exchange and Third-Party Resellers

In 2026, specialized baby gear resale sites like **Stork Exchange** or **GoodBuy Gear** often have "Open Box" or "Like New" Lovevery kits. These are kits that were returned or had damaged packaging. You can frequently find these for 30-40% off the original price, and they are verified for safety and cleanliness by the platform.
