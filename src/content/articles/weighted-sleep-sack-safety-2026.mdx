## The 2026 AAP Guidelines: What Has Changed?

The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) has historically been cautious about any "extra" items in the crib. In 2026, the guidance focuses on three specific factors for weighted products:

1.  **Weight Distribution:** The weight should never be concentrated over the baby's chest or abdomen, as this could interfere with breathing or rib cage expansion.
2.  **Total Weight:** The total weight of the garment should not exceed 10% of the baby's body weight.
3.  **Mobility:** A baby must be able to roll over, sit up, and move freely while wearing the sack.

**The Consensus:** While the AAP remains conservative, they have acknowledged that products meeting strict weight-to-body-mass ratios and featuring "gravity-distributed" designs can be used safely when following manufacturer instructions.

## What Makes a Weighted Sleep Sack "Safe"?

If you are considering a weighted solution, do not buy a generic or DIY product. Look for these four safety markers:

### 1. Small-Pellet Distribution
Safe sacks use micro-beads sewn into small, individual pockets across the entire front of the garment. This prevents the weight from shifting or bunching up over the chest.

### 2. Breathable Fabrics
Overheating is a major risk factor for SIDS. In 2026, the best products use bamboo or moisture-wicking cotton to ensure the baby stays at an optimal temperature.

### 3. "Gentle Pressure" Design
The goal is a "hug," not a "heavy blanket." The pressure should be subtle enough that the baby barely notices it until they enter a deep sleep state.

### 4. Safety Certifications
Ensure the product is CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission) compliant and has undergone third-party testing for lead, phthalates, and flammability.
