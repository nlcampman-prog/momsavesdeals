## Beyond LH Strips: Why Quantitative Data Matters

Most traditional ovulation tests only track Luteinizing Hormone (LH) and give you a binary "Yes" or "No" result based on a threshold. While LH surges are a good indicator of impending ovulation, they don't tell the whole story. Many women—especially those with PCOS or irregular cycles—experience multiple surges or "false starts," making it difficult to pin down the actual fertile window.

**Mira** tracks three (and sometimes four) key hormones:
1.  **LH (Luteinizing Hormone):** Predicts your ovulation peak.
2.  **E3G (Estrogen):** Identifies your full 6-day fertile window by rising before the LH surge.
3.  **PdG (Progesterone):** Confirms that ovulation actually occurred (crucial for knowing if your body is ready for pregnancy).
4.  **FSH (Follicle-Stimulating Hormone):** Available on specific wands to help assess overall ovarian reserve.

By tracking these together, Mira gives you a complete, data-backed picture of your cycle.

## The Mira Hardware: Small but Mighty

The Mira Analyzer is a palm-sized, sleek device that uses **fluorescence-based technology**. This is the same diagnostic technology used in hospital laboratories, shrunk down for home use. 

### How it works:
1.  **The Wand:** You dip a disposable Mira Wand into a urine sample for about 10-20 seconds.
2.  **The Analyzer:** Insert the wand into the Analyzer.
3.  **The Data:** Wait about 10-15 minutes (perfect time for your morning coffee). The device uses Bluetooth to sync the data directly to the Mira App.
4.  **The Curve:** The app displays your exact hormone concentrations and charts them on a personalized curve, showing you exactly where you are in your cycle.

## Mira vs. Inito: Which is better?

This is the most common question in the TTC community. Both Mira and Inito provide quantitative hormone data, but there are key differences:

*   **Standalone Device vs. Phone Attachment:** Mira is a standalone analyzer that syncs via Bluetooth. Inito is a clip-on attachment that requires your phone to be physically attached to the reader. If you have a bulky phone case or upgrade your phone frequently, Mira's standalone device is often more convenient.
*   **Hormone Range:** Mira is generally regarded as having a wider detection range for hormones, which is critical for women with very high or very low baseline levels that might "max out" other tests.
*   **The App:** The Mira App is exceptionally polished, offering deep insights, cycle predictions that get smarter over time, and a supportive community of users.

## FAQ: Mira Fertility Review

### Can I use Mira if I have PCOS?
Yes! Mira is specifically designed to handle the hormone fluctuations common in PCOS. By showing you the numerical value of your hormones, you can see the difference between a "false" surge and your actual peak.

### Does Mira confirm pregnancy?
While Mira tracks progesterone (PdG), which remains high during pregnancy, it is not a pregnancy test. You should still use a standard hCG test (which Mira also offers) to confirm.

### How accurate is it?
Mira is 99% accurate in detecting hormone surges, making it as reliable as a lab-based urine test.
