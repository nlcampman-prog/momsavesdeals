## Why Ergonomics Matter

A good baby carrier should support the "M-position," where the baby's knees are higher than their bottom. This ensures healthy hip development and prevents hip dysplasia. For the parent, look for wide, padded straps and a supportive waistband to distribute the baby's weight evenly.

## What to Look for in 2026

1.  **Breathability:** Especially important if you plan on wearing your baby for long periods or live in a warm climate.
2.  **Adjustability:** If multiple caregivers will be using the carrier, make sure it’s easy to adjust the straps quickly.
3.  **Ease of Cleaning:** Babies are messy. Choose a carrier that is machine washable!
