## Why Ergonomics Matter

When choosing a carrier, the "M-position" is non-negotiable. This means the baby's knees should be higher than their bottom, creating an "M" shape with their legs. This position supports healthy hip development and prevents hip dysplasia.

For the parent, a good carrier should distribute the baby's weight evenly across the hips and shoulders, preventing back strain during long carries.

## What to Look for in 2026

1.  **Fabric Technology:** Look for moisture-wicking and antimicrobial fabrics, especially for summer babies.
2.  **Ease of Use:** If you can't put it on by yourself in under 60 seconds, you probably won't use it.
3.  **Longevity:** Some carriers require an infant insert, while others (like the Ergo Omni) adjust as the baby grows.
